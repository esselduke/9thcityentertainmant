Who we are and what this policy covers.

9TH CITY ENTERTAINMENT ("9TH CITY," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information with care and respect. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, interact with our services, or communicate with us.

Who We Are: 9TH CITY ENTERTAINMENT is a music entertainment company operating in Accra and Toronto, providing artist management, music production, and brand development services.

Scope: This policy applies to all information collected through:

  • Our website (9thcityent.com)
  • Email communications
  • Contact forms and submission portals
  • Social media interactions
  • Events and promotional activities

By using our services, you consent to the data practices described in this policy.

Categories of information we may collect from you.

Personal Information You Provide:

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, phone number, mailing address
  • Professional Information: Company name, job title, industry
  • Communication Content: Messages sent through contact forms, emails, or inquiries
  • Artist Submissions: Demo materials, bio information, links to work, social media handles
  • Newsletter Signups: Email address and name for mailing list subscriptions
  • Event Registration: RSVP information for events and showcases

Automatically Collected Information:

  • Device Information: IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent on site, click patterns, referring URLs
  • Location Data: General geographic location based on IP address
  • Cookies & Tracking: Information collected through cookies and similar technologies (see our Cookie Policy)

Third-Party Information:

  • Analytics data from Google Analytics, social media platforms
  • Publicly available information from social media profiles you connect with us
  • Information from streaming platforms if you interact with our artists' content

Purposes for which we process your information.

We use collected information for the following purposes:

Service Delivery & Operations:

  • Responding to inquiries and providing customer support
  • Processing artist submissions and collaboration requests
  • Sending newsletters and promotional materials (with consent)
  • Managing event registrations and ticket sales

Communication:

  • Sending important updates about our services
  • Marketing communications about new releases, events, and opportunities
  • Responding to your questions and requests

Analytics & Improvement:

  • Analyzing website usage to improve user experience
  • Understanding audience demographics and preferences
  • Testing new features and optimizing content
  • Conducting market research and trend analysis

Legal & Compliance:

  • Complying with legal obligations and regulations
  • Protecting our rights and preventing fraud or abuse
  • Enforcing our Terms of Service and other policies
  • Responding to legal requests from authorities

Legal grounds for processing your personal data.

We process your personal data based on the following legal grounds:

Consent: When you provide explicit consent for specific purposes, such as newsletter subscriptions or marketing communications. You may withdraw consent at any time.

Contractual Necessity: When processing is necessary to fulfill a contract with you or take steps at your request before entering into a contract.

Legitimate Interests: When we have a legitimate business interest that is not overridden by your privacy rights, such as:

  • Improving our services and user experience
  • Preventing fraud and ensuring security
  • Direct marketing to existing customers
  • Analytics and business intelligence

Legal Obligation: When we must process data to comply with legal requirements, such as tax laws or responding to valid legal requests.

Who we share your data with and why.

We may share your information with the following categories of third parties:

Service Providers:

  • Web Hosting: CDN and hosting providers for website infrastructure
  • Analytics: Google Analytics for website traffic and user behavior analysis (Google Privacy Policy)
  • Email Services: SendGrid, Mailchimp, or similar for email communications (SendGrid Privacy)
  • Payment Processors: Stripe, PayPal for processing payments (when applicable)

Streaming & Media Platforms:

  • YouTube: For embedded video content (YouTube Privacy)
  • Spotify: For embedded music players (Spotify Privacy)
  • SoundCloud: For audio streaming (SoundCloud Privacy)

Social Media Platforms:

  • Instagram, Twitter/X, Facebook, TikTok for social media integrations and analytics

Legal & Compliance:

  • Law enforcement or regulatory authorities when required by law
  • Legal advisors and professional service providers
  • Parties involved in business transactions (mergers, acquisitions)

We ensure all third-party processors maintain appropriate data protection standards and only use your data for specified purposes.

How we use cookies and tracking technologies.

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience and analyze site usage. Cookies are small text files stored on your device.

Types of Cookies We Use:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for basic site functionality (cannot be disabled)
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our site
  • Marketing Cookies: Track your visit across websites for advertising purposes
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and settings

Manage Your Cookie Preferences: For detailed information about cookies and to manage your preferences, visit our Cookie Choices page.

Third-Party Tracking: Some third-party services may set their own cookies when you interact with embedded content (e.g., YouTube videos, Spotify players). These are governed by the respective third-party privacy policies.

How long we keep your information.

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law.

Retention Periods:

  • Contact Form Submissions: 3 years from last contact
  • Newsletter Subscriptions: Until you unsubscribe, then 30 days
  • Artist Submissions: 2 years from submission date
  • Analytics Data: 26 months (Google Analytics default)
  • Financial Records: 7 years (as required by law)
  • Marketing Consent: Until withdrawn, reviewed annually

Archival & Deletion: After the retention period expires, we securely delete or anonymize your data. Some information may be retained in backup systems for up to 90 days after deletion.

Legal Holds: We may retain data longer if required for legal proceedings, investigations, or regulatory requirements.

Your privacy rights and how to exercise them.

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right to Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.

Right to Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.

Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances.

Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format and transfer it to another service.

Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.

Right to Restrict Processing: Request limitation of processing in certain situations.

Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.

Right to Lodge a Complaint: File a complaint with your local data protection authority.

How to Exercise Your Rights:

To make a data request or exercise any of your rights, contact us at:
Email: privacy@9thcityent.com

Subject line: "Data Subject Request - [Your Name]"

We will respond to your request within 30 days. You may need to provide identification to verify your identity.

Unsubscribe from Marketing: Click the "unsubscribe" link in any marketing email, or contact privacy@9thcityent.com.

How we protect your information.

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data:

Technical Safeguards:

  • Encryption: SSL/TLS encryption for data transmission
  • Access Controls: Role-based access restrictions and authentication
  • Regular Backups: Secure, encrypted backup systems
  • Security Monitoring: Continuous monitoring for threats and vulnerabilities
  • Firewalls: Network security barriers and intrusion detection

Organizational Safeguards:

  • Staff training on data protection and security
  • Confidentiality agreements with employees and contractors
  • Regular security audits and assessments
  • Incident response and breach notification procedures

Data Breach Notification: In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and relevant authorities within 72 hours as required by law.

Your Responsibility: Please use strong passwords, keep your credentials secure, and notify us immediately if you suspect unauthorized access to your account.

How we handle cross-border data transfers.

9TH CITY operates internationally, with operations in Accra (Ghana) and Toronto (Canada). Your personal data may be transferred to, stored, and processed in jurisdictions different from your country of residence.

Data Transfer Mechanisms:

  • We ensure adequate safeguards are in place for international transfers
  • We use standard contractual clauses approved by relevant authorities
  • We work with service providers who comply with international data protection standards

Third-Party Transfers: Some of our service providers (e.g., Google Analytics, email services) may process data in the United States or other countries. These providers have certified compliance with relevant data protection frameworks.

Privacy protection for children and minors.

Our services are not directed to individuals under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Age Requirements:

  • Users must be at least 13 years old to use our services
  • Users between 13 and 18 must have parental or guardian consent

Parental Rights: If you believe we have collected information from a child under 13, please contact us immediately at privacy@9thcityent.com and we will promptly delete the information.

Parental Consent: Parents or guardians may request access to, review, or delete their child's personal information by contacting us.

How we notify you of policy updates.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, legal requirements, or business operations.

Notification of Changes:

  • We will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page
  • For material changes, we will provide prominent notice on our website
  • We may send email notifications to registered users
  • We will obtain fresh consent where required by law

Review: We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.

Privacy Team
