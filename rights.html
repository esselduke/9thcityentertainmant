How to request permission to use our content.

If you would like to use 9TH CITY content in a way that requires permission, please submit a formal request to our legal team.

Types of Licensing Available:

  • Sync Licenses: For use of music in film, TV, commercials, video games, or online content
  • Mechanical Licenses: For physical or digital reproduction of compositions
  • Public Performance Licenses: For venues, events, and broadcasts
  • Brand Partnership Licenses: For commercial collaborations and endorsements
  • Press & Media Use: For high-resolution assets, interviews, or features

How to Submit a Request:

  1. Email legal@9thcityent.com with subject line: "License Request - [Your Name/Company]"
  2. Include the following information:
    • Your name and contact information
    • Organization or company name (if applicable)
    • Description of intended use
    • Specific content you wish to use (song title, video, image, etc.)
    • Territory and duration of use
    • Distribution method (theatrical, broadcast, online, etc.)
    • Budget and compensation offered
  3. Wait for our response (typically 5-10 business days)
  4. If approved, sign licensing agreement and provide payment

Expedited Requests: For time-sensitive projects, please mark your email as "URGENT" and provide your deadline. We will do our best to accommodate rush requests, though additional fees may apply.

Press & Media: For press inquiries, media features, or interview requests, contact press@9thcityent.com.

How to report copyright infringement.

9TH CITY ENTERTAINMENT respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects users to do the same. We comply with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) and will respond to valid takedown notices.

If Your Content Appears on Our Site Without Permission:

If you believe your copyrighted work has been used on our website without authorization, you may submit a DMCA takedown notice.

Required Information for DMCA Notice:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed
  3. Identification of the material that is claimed to be infringing (with URL or location)
  4. Your contact information (address, phone number, email)
  5. A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use is not authorized
  6. A statement that the information in the notice is accurate
  7. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner

Submit DMCA Notices To:

DMCA Agent
9TH CITY ENTERTAINMENT
Email: legal@9thcityent.com
Subject: "DMCA Takedown Notice"

If 9TH CITY Content is Infringed:

If you discover unauthorized use of 9TH CITY content on another platform, please report it to us at report@9thcityent.com with:

  • URL or location of the infringing content
  • Description of the content and how it's being used
  • Platform or website where it appears
  • Screenshots or documentation (if available)

Counter-Notices: If you believe a DMCA notice was filed in error, you may submit a counter-notice. Please contact legal@9thcityent.com for counter-notice procedures.

False Claims: Submitting a false or fraudulent DMCA notice may result in legal liability. Only submit a notice if you have a genuine belief that your copyright has been infringed.

Legal consequences for rights violations.

9TH CITY ENTERTAINMENT actively monitors and enforces its intellectual property rights. We take all violations seriously and will pursue appropriate legal remedies.

Enforcement Actions May Include:

  • Cease and Desist Letters: Formal demand to stop unauthorized use
  • Takedown Requests: Filing DMCA notices with platforms hosting infringing content
  • Account Suspensions: Requesting suspension or termination of accounts engaged in infringement
  • Civil Litigation: Filing lawsuits for damages, injunctive relief, and attorney's fees
  • Criminal Referrals: Reporting commercial-scale infringement to law enforcement

Potential Damages:

Copyright infringement can result in statutory damages of up to $150,000 per work infringed, plus actual damages, lost profits, and recovery of legal fees. Trademark infringement can result in injunctions, disgorgement of profits, and monetary damages.

Resolution Without Litigation:

We prefer to resolve disputes amicably when possible. If you receive a notice from us, please respond promptly to discuss resolution options, which may include:

  • Immediate removal of infringing content
  • Negotiation of a retroactive license
  • Settlement agreements
  • Written assurances of future compliance

Good Faith Cooperation: If you inadvertently infringed our rights and respond cooperatively, we may be willing to resolve the matter without pursuing damages. Prompt action and good faith communication are key.
